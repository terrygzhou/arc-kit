The UAE Cabinet just gave Chief AI Officers the hardest deadline in government IT.

50% of federal sectors and services on agentic AI. Within 24 months. Personal performance assessment attached.

Most teams will miss it. Not because the AI doesn't work, but because the architecture governance can't keep up.

Here is the 90 day playbook to establish the cadence that makes the deadline arithmetically possible.

DAYS 1 TO 5. FOUNDATION.

Install ArcKit on day 1. Configure your federal entity name and OFFICIAL-SENSITIVE classification on day 2. Run /arckit.principles on day 4. Pick three pathfinder services on day 5. The conventional approach to principle ratification takes 6 to 12 weeks. ArcKit takes 5 days.

WEEK 2. FIRST PATHFINDER.

/arckit.requirements generates the BR/FR/NFR/INT/DR set against your service. /arckit.stakeholders maps influence and interest. /arckit.risks builds the register. Three artefacts on disk in 5 days, not 4 weeks.

WEEKS 3 TO 4. DATA AND INTEGRATION.

/arckit.data-model from your DR requirements. /arckit.integration from your INT requirements. Both citation-traceable, classified, versioned. 5 days, not 3 weeks.

WEEKS 5 TO 6. RESEARCH AND REUSE.

/arckit.research runs autonomous vendor evaluation overnight. /arckit.gov-reuse queries open government code repositories for code you can adopt instead of build. The cloud knowledge MCPs are interrogated by /arckit.aws-research, /arckit.azure-research, /arckit.gcp-research. The full procurement evidence base in 5 days, not 6 weeks.

WEEKS 7 TO 8. DECISIONS AND BUSINESS CASE.

/arckit.adr per material decision (typically 12 to 15 per pathfinder). /arckit.sobc generates the five-case Strategic Outline Business Case from every prior artefact. SOBC delivery in 4 days, not 4 weeks.

WEEKS 9 TO 10. SITUATIONAL AWARENESS.

/arckit.wardley generates the OnlineWardleyMaps view. /arckit.framework codifies the pathfinder's reusable patterns for the rest of the portfolio. Most programmes never produce a framework artefact, which is why stage three of their delivery never compresses.

WEEKS 11 TO 12. PATHFINDERS 2 AND 3.

Each subsequent pathfinder costs 60% of the first. Two pairs of architects, parallel delivery, two more services on the table.

WEEK 13. TASKFORCE REVIEW.

Three pathfinder services fully articulated. Federal principles ratified. Framework artefact in hand. Configured toolchain any new architect can pick up in an afternoon.

You have spent 90 days. You have produced what most federal entities produce in 9 months.

THE LEVERAGE POINT.

ArcKit moves the architect's time from drafting to judging. Every command produces a strong starting draft against a versioned template, classified, citation-anchored, and integrated with the rest of the project's artefacts through stable IDs. The architect's contribution is editorial judgement, not prose.

That redistribution is what the 24 month deadline mathematically requires. Without it, the deadline is missed. With it, the deadline becomes a planning constraint, not an existential risk.

The decree gave you the deadline. ArcKit gives you the cadence.

Bonus: this entire artefact set, including the playbook itself, is built using agentic AI. The technology the Cabinet has mandated for service delivery is the technology that produces the governance trail behind it.
